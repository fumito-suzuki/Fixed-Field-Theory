## Z+18.1 Definition of the Projection Operator $f$

The observer does not access Ψ directly, but receives a receptor-specific
projection $C$:

$$
C(x,y,t) = (f\Psi)(x,y,t).
$$

Formally, $f$ is a **bounded linear operator** defined by:

$$
(f\Psi)(x,y,t)
=
\int_{\mathbb{R}} K(x,y,z;t)\, \Psi(x,y,z,t)\, dz.
$$

Where:

- $K$ is the **projection kernel**, encoding receptor sensitivity.  
- For visual perception, $K$ is sharply peaked at the retino-optic Z-axis.  
- For other modalities, $K$ spans distinct subspaces.

### Properties of the kernel $K$

1. **Normalization**  
$$
\int_{\mathbb{R}} K(x,y,z;t)\,dz = 1.
$$

2. **Non-negativity**  
$$
K(x,y,z;t) \ge 0.
$$

3. **Compact support or exponential decay**  
ensuring finite energy projection.

4. **Time-locality**  
$K$ acts within the τ-window defined by phase-time geometry.

Under these conditions:

$$
f: L^2(\mathbb{R}^3) \rightarrow L^2(\mathbb{R}^2)
\quad\text{is bounded and continuous.}
$$

## Z+18.2 Adjoint Operator $f^\dagger$

The adjoint operator satisfies:

$$
\langle f\Psi, C \rangle_{XY}
=
\langle \Psi, f^\dagger C \rangle_{XYZ}.
$$

This yields:

$$
(f^{\dagger} C)(x,y,z,t)
=
K(x,y,z;t)\,C(x,y,t).
$$

Thus $f^\dagger$ embeds a 2D pattern back into 3D by “extruding” the receptor
pattern along the Z-axis using the same kernel.

## Z+18.3 Reconstruction Operator $G = f^\dagger f$

Define:

$$
G := f^{\dagger} f.
$$

Explicitly:

$$
(G\Psi)(x,y,z,t)
=
K(x,y,z;t)
\int_{\mathbb{R}} K(x,y,z';t)\,\Psi(x,y,z',t)\,dz'.
$$

Properties:

- **positive**  
  $$
  \langle \Psi, G\Psi \rangle \ge 0.
  $$

- **self-adjoint**  
  $$
  G^\dagger = G.
  $$

- **bounded**  
  $$
  \|G\| \le \|K\|^2.
  $$

- **trace-class under mild conditions**  
  (compact support or exponential decay in Z).

Thus $G$ satisfies all the axioms of a **quantum density operator**.

## Z+18.4 Maximum-Entropy Extension as the Definition of ρ

FFT defines the internal information state ρ as the **maximum entropy**
extension of observable C.

Formally:

$$
\rho = \arg\max_{\rho}
\left\{
S[\rho]
= -\int \rho \log \rho \, dV
\right\}
$$

subject to:

- the XY marginal constraint  
  $$
  \int \rho(x,y,z)\,dz = |C(x,y)|^2,
  $$

- and geometric moment constraints that encode T-fiber curvature.

The result:

$$
\rho = f^\dagger f \Psi = G\Psi.
$$

Thus **MaxEnt reconstruction and operator reconstruction coincide**.

## Z+18.6 Multi-Modal Projection: Visual, Auditory, Tactile C-Wave

In general, observable data may have multiple channels:

$$
C = (C_{\mathrm{vis}}, C_{\mathrm{aud}}, C_{\mathrm{som}}, \dots).
$$

Each modality has its own projection kernel $K_i$:

$$
C_i = f_i \Psi.
$$

Define the composite projection:

$$
f_{\mathrm{multi}}
=
\begin{pmatrix}
f_1 \\ f_2 \\ \vdots
\end{pmatrix}.
$$

Then:

$$
G_{\mathrm{multi}}
=
f_{\mathrm{multi}}^\dagger f_{\mathrm{multi}}
=
\sum_i f_i^\dagger f_i.
$$

Thus **multi-sensory integration** corresponds to **summed density operators**.

This is consistent with neurophysiological models of multisensory fusion.
