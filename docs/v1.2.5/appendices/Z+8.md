# Appendix Z+8 — The Three-Layer Ontology of Fixed-Field Theory

*(Fixed-Field Theory v1.2.x Supplemental Material)*

## 1. Purpose and Scope

This appendix provides a **strict ontological classification** of the three
distinct layers that constitute reality in Fixed-Field Theory (FFT):

- the **T-wave** (causal field),
- the **C-wave** (interference / information field),
- and **ψ** (the generated phenomenon).

Its purpose is to prevent category confusion between fields, waves, and
generated geometry, and to fix the interpretation required to correctly read
Sections 1–7 of the main text.

This appendix does **not** introduce new dynamics.
It formalizes distinctions already used implicitly throughout FFT.

---

## 2. Background (χδ–ψ Framework)

FFT is founded on the χδ principle (Appendix Z+0):

$$
\psi = \chi\delta.
$$

Here:

- χ encodes causal curvature along the Z / τ direction,
- δ encodes interference density on delayed projection planes,
- ψ is the **appearance field** generated at their interaction.

The main text repeatedly emphasizes that **ψ is not a physical wave**.
This appendix clarifies *why that statement is structurally necessary*.

## 3. Main Derivation / Model

### 3.1 T-wave — Continuous Causal Field (Matter)

The **T-wave** is the persistent causal structure of the fixed field.
It corresponds to *matter itself*, not to a signal propagating through matter.

Properties:

- continuous and non-discrete,
- non-directional and non-refractive,
- capable of storing irreversible Z-history,
- acts as a receptor for interference,
- determines inertia, solidity, and persistence.

Formally, the T-wave corresponds to χ:

$$
\text{T-wave} \;\equiv\; \chi(x,y,z).
$$

The T-wave exists independently of observation.

---

### 3.2 C-wave — Interference Field (Information)

The **C-wave** is a directional interference field that carries information
but no causal curvature.

Examples include electromagnetic radiation of all frequencies.

Fundamental constraint:

$$
\chi_{\text{C-wave}} = 0.
$$

Characteristics:

- directional and refractive,
- frequency-dependent,
- capable of superposition and interference,
- requires a T-wave receptor to produce an event,
- corresponds to the interference operator δ.

Formally:

$$
\text{C-wave} \;\equiv\; \delta(x,y,t).
$$

C-waves do not create reality; they probe it.

---

### 3.3 ψ — Generated Phenomenon (Not a Wave)

The appearance field ψ is defined by operator composition:

$$
\psi = \chi\delta.
$$

This implies:

- χ provides causal grounding,
- δ provides interference structure,
- ψ is the **phenomenon that appears** at their contact.

ψ corresponds to:

- visible surfaces and boundaries,
- texture, gloss, and shading,
- apparent depth and occlusion,
- mass-density gradients as experienced.

ψ does **not** propagate.
ψ has **no intrinsic frequency**.
ψ does **not** exist without both χ and δ.

Therefore:

$$
\psi \neq \text{wave}.
$$

ψ is a generated geometry, not a physical entity.
