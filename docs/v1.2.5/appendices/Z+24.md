## 1. Purpose and Scope

This appendix defines the **fractal Laplacian operator $\Delta^{D}$** within
Fixed-Field Theory (FFT).

- provide a precise and FFT-consistent definition of $\Delta^{D}$,
- clarify its conceptual role relative to χ, δ, and ψ,
- establish a mathematically rigorous and numerically implementable form,
- prevent misinterpretations of $\Delta^{D}$ as a modification of physical space.

The operator $\Delta^{D}$ is defined as an **observational operator**
acting on the appearance field

$$
\psi = \chi\delta.
$$

FFT distinguishes strictly between:

- χ — causal depth on the T-fiber side (non-observable),

$$

- $\Delta^{D}$ does not modify the causal substrate χ,

- $\Delta^{D}$ modifies how interference structures are **projected and weighted
  across spatial scales**.

$\Delta^{D}$ is therefore classified as an **observational operator**, not a
dynamical one.

### 3.2 Mathematical Definition (Riesz Form)

To ensure compatibility with periodic boundary conditions,
spectral methods, and scale invariance,
$\Delta^{D}$ is defined as the **Riesz fractional Laplacian**.

Let $\mathcal{F}$ denote the Fourier transform.
Then

$$
\boxed{
\mathcal{F}\!\left[\Delta^{D}\psi\right](\mathbf{k})=
-|\mathbf{k}|^{D}\,\hat{\psi}(\mathbf{k})
}
$$

where:

- $\mathbf{k}$ is the spatial wavevector,
- $D \in \mathbb{R}^{+}$ is the fractional order,
- $\hat{\psi}$ is the Fourier transform of $\psi$.

This operator is:

- nonlocal in physical space,
- diagonal in Fourier space,
- naturally compatible with FFT-based numerical solvers.

### 3.3 Relation to the Standard Laplacian

The limiting relation

$$
\lim_{D \to 2} \Delta^{D}\psi = \Delta\psi
$$

holds identically.

Thus $\Delta^{D}$ does not replace the standard Laplacian.
It generalizes it continuously, ensuring compatibility with classical physics
and smooth reduction to known models.
