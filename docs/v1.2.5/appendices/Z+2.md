\[

\[

Only their product ψ = χδ generates the T-fiber geometry that manifests as
mass, perception, and events.

\[

- entangle: δ reshapes χ by altering which causal branches interfere constructively.  
- expand: χ amplifies or suppresses δ, making some interference patterns persist

Symbolically:

\[
\chi\delta
\quad\leadsto\quad
(\text{curvature entangled with interference})

\]

“Entangle–expand” therefore captures the fact that χ and δ are mutually
defining:

- form cannot exist without the interference structure that selects it;  
- interference cannot be observed without curvature that stabilizes it.

## Z+2.5 Fixed Points and the Vacuum Structure

The χδ operation exhibits three qualitatively different regimes:

1. **Trivial vacuum**  
   \[
   \chi = 0, \quad \delta = 0 \quad\Rightarrow\quad \psi = 0.
   \]  
   No curvature, no interference, no structure.

2. **Curvature-only vacuum**  
   \[
   \chi \neq 0, \quad \delta = 0 \quad\Rightarrow\quad \psi = 0.
   \]  
   Latent causal potential; geometrically nontrivial but unexpressed.  
   This regime is related to “dark curvature” (dark-matter–like behavior).

3. **Interference-only vacuum**  
   \[
   \chi = 0, \quad \delta \neq 0 \quad\Rightarrow\quad \psi = 0.
   \]  
   Fluctuating patterns with no lasting effect: pure, non-binding emptiness.

Only when both χ and δ are non-zero:

\[
\chi \neq 0, \quad \delta \neq 0,
\]

does ψ become nontrivial and the T-fiber field Ψ acquire observable structure.
In this sense, **form and emptiness are inseparable requirements for reality**.
