## Neutrinos as Ultra-Low-Density C-Waves in FFT

weakly-interacting particles that pass through matter with almost no effect.
Fixed-Field Theory (FFT) reinterprets this behavior in terms of the fundamental
information fields:

- **T-wave**: first-order information (matter field; receptive)
- **C-wave**: second-order information (directional; transmissive)
- **ψ-wave**: generated structure field (surface geometry)

Neutrinos occupy a unique position in this taxonomy.

# Z+19.1 Neutrinos Are Extremely Low-Density C-Waves

FFT identifies neutrinos not as massive particles but as:

$$
\boxed{
\textbf{ultra-low-density, nearly straight-propagating C-waves.}
}
$$

Their properties follow directly:

### 1. **Almost no curvature sensitivity (χ ≈ 0)**  
T-wave curvature does not significantly bend or absorb them.  
Thus they traverse vast amounts of matter without being “observed.”

### 2. **Minimal interference (δ ≈ 0 except under rare conditions)**  
Since C-wave superposition is weak, they do not form stable ψ-wave boundaries.

### 3. **Directionality is nearly perfect**  
Because δ-structure is extremely low, neutrinos propagate close to straight
geodesics through the T-field.

This explains why neutrinos pass through:

- planets  
- stars  
- human bodies  
- dense materials  

as if they do not exist.

$$
